Devising dreams. Creating wonders.

In the annals of Mahinda College, few names shine as brightly as Frank Lee Woodward. A visionary educator and scholar, Woodward’s leadership not only strengthened the foundation of Mahinda but also shaped its identity as a beacon of knowledge, discipline, and cultural revival.

His tenure marked a golden era, one that transformed the institution into a center for academic and spiritual enrichment. To understand the legacy of Mahinda is to explore the remarkable contributions of Woodward, whose devotion to education and Buddhist philosophy left an indelible mark on Sri Lanka’s intellectual and social landscape.
